Robot Specifications

2019 | Robot

Robot Overview

The Beak Squad 2019 robot was built to accomplish all the major tasks required for competition in an alliance.  It collects cargo and hatches; climbs to the third level of the habitat; and can place game peices on the top level of the rocket.

  • Our robot will score as many game pieces into the rockets as possible 
  • The 4028 robot is a third level climber
  • Our code optimizes path choices to maximize efficiency in piece placements

Sandstorm

  • Autonomous programming helps us follow paths more efficiently and allows our team to score multiple game pieces without potentially using vision or controllers
  • The programs use path following (dynamic path generation) with waypoint-based path construction, nonlinear state estimation
  • The autonomous mode has an adaptive pure pursuit controller

Vision

  • The vision system calculates and models different angles that the robot uses to position relatively
  • Robot features a built-in camera with LED/processor called The Lime Light
  • The Lime Light looks for “reflective tape” by shining a specific light
  • The Lime Light also looks for a specific reflection that can create shapes and patterns to create a target
  • Vision feature useful both in Sandstorm and Teleoperation

Bucket & Beak

  • Collects, contains, and places hatches on target slot
  • Two plates operate pnuematically
  • The first plate moves the “beak” forward to place hatches
  • The second plate opens and closes to clasp the hatches
  • This part of the robot helps us earn a rank point as well as tons of points
  • Two pneumatic cylinders are activated to grab the cargo
  • Ten wheels attached to a shaft, driven by a bag motor, intake and place the cargo

Elevator & Climber

  • Allows scoring in top and bottom rocket with high consistancy, effectivity, and speed
  • Elevator cascades vertically
  • Similar to a rope and pully system
  • Climbing allows our team to get a guaranteed rank point and to all 3 levels possible to climb
  • The climber is on a separate elevator that is contra to the other elevator
  • There is a long foot that goes under the center of gravity so the robot does not fall over
  • Climbing elevator is positioned behind the center of gravity, so the robot does not trip while climbing

Chassis

  • Acts as the primary driving mechanism for the robot
  • Utilized a kit chassis to prioritize other functions and allow the control team maximum time to work
  • The mechanism uses a gearbox in the middle of the frame with timing belts to control 3 wheels
  • Wheels run on CIM motors

Our Achievements

Worlds

Years Achieved: 2018, 2017, 2016, 2015

Worlds

Our team found victory at State and advanced to the Worlds Competition four years in a row. Worlds is the culmination of our work in one week-long competition.

Finalist

Years Achieved: 2018, 2017, 2016

Finalist

The Beak Squad has earned the title of "Finalist" for being in the winning alliance at a competition!

State Champion

Years Achieved: 2018, 2016

State Champion

CHCA Beak Squad wins 2016 Ohio State FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) Championship as the Alliance Captain and 1st Place Alliance, remaining undefeated for the entire tournament!

Innovation in Controls

Years Achieved: 2018, 2017, 2016

Innovation in Controls

The Beak Squad is one of the few winners of the Innovation in Controls award, given for an innovative control system or application of control components – electrical, mechanical, or software – which provide unique machine functions.

Industrial Design

Years Achieved: 2017, 2016

Industrial Design

Our form and function in robot design showed effectively and efficiency in addressing that year's game challenge.

Autonomous Award

Years Achieved: 2018

Autonomous Award Sponsored by Ford, Awarded at Worlds

Autonomous celebrates that we have demonstrated consistent, reliable, high-performance robot operation during autonomously managed actions. Only 6 teams, including the Beak Squad, were awarded in 2018 based on the robot’s ability to sense its surroundings, position itself or onboard mechanisms appropriately, and execute tasks.

Quality Award

Years Achieved: 2017

Quality Award

Our 2017 machine was awarded for robustness in concept and fabrication.

FedEx Innovation Challenge

Years Achieved: 2015

Fed Ex Innovation Challenge

CHCA Beak Squad took 1st Place in the 2015 FedEx Innovation Challenge, recognizing intuitive, creative, and overall innovative design.
